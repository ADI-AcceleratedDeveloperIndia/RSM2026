TRAFFIC AND ROAD SIGNS - LLR MOCK TEST
========================================

Traffic and road signs are simple representations of rules that are put in place by the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways. They provide important information to drivers as well as other road users, and one must adhere to all these rules to ensure a smooth and safe drive. It is important to have a good understanding of road safety signs and traffic signals in order to being safe on the road.

There are so many road and traffic signs, each one having its own purpose. They have been divided into three categories, which are Mandatory, Cautionary and Informatory Signs.


MANDATORY SIGNS
===============

These road signals and signs are used to make road users aware of specific laws and regulations that must be followed.

1. Stop
   This sign indicates that the vehicle must come to a complete halt.

2. Give Way
   This sign indicates that the driver must give way to traffic on the main road.

3. No Entry
   This sign indicates that entry is prohibited for all vehicles.

4. One Way

5. No Left Turn

6. No Right Turn

7. No U-Turn

9. Speed Limit

10. No Horn

11. No Parking

12. No Stopping

13. Compulsory Turn Left

20. Compulsory Keep Right

CAUTIONARY SIGNS
================

These signs are used to warn road users of potential hazards or dangerous conditions ahead.

1. Right Hand Curve
   This sign indicates that there is a right-hand curve ahead on the road.

2. Left Hand Curve
   This sign indicates that there is a left-hand curve ahead on the road.

3. Right Hair Pin Bend
   This sign indicates that there is a sharp right turn ahead.

4. Left Hair Pin Bend
   This sign indicates that there is a sharp left turn ahead.

5. Right Reverse Bend
   This sign indicates that there is a reverse right bend ahead.

6. Left Reverse Bend
   This sign indicates that there is a reverse left bend ahead.

7. Steep Ascent
   This sign indicates that there is a steep uphill section ahead.

8. Steep Descent
   This sign indicates that there is a steep downhill section ahead.

9. Narrow Road Ahead
   This sign indicates that the road ahead becomes narrow.

10. Road Wideness Ahead
    This sign indicates that the road ahead becomes wider.

11. Narrow Bridge
    This sign indicates that there is a narrow bridge ahead.

12. Slippery Road
    This sign indicates that the road surface is slippery.

13. Loose Gravel
    This sign indicates that loose earth or gravel keeps falling on the road.

14. Cycle Crossing
    This sign indicates that there is a cycle crossing ahead.

15. Pedestrian Crossing
    This sign indicates that there is a pedestrian crossing ahead.

16. School Ahead
    This sign indicates that there is a school ahead, so drivers should be cautious.

17. Men at Work
    This sign indicates that there are workers on the road ahead.

18. Cattle
    This sign indicates that cattle may be present on the road.

19. Falling Rocks
    This sign indicates that the road ahead is prone to falling rocks, especially in hilly areas.

20. Ferry
    This sign indicates that there is a ferry service ahead to cross the river.

21. Cross Road
    This sign indicates that there is a crossing of roads ahead.

22. Gap in Median
    This sign indicates that there is a gap in the divider of the road.

23. Side Road Right
    This sign indicates that there is a right side turn on the road in addition to straight movement.

24. Side Road Left
    This sign indicates that there is a left side turn on the road.

25. Y-Intersections
    This sign depicts the actual formation of road ahead.

26. T-Intersection
    This sign indicates that straight ahead the traffic has to either turn left or right.

27. Staggered Intersection
    These signs indicate that there are left and right turns available on the straight road.

28. Major Road Ahead

29. Roundabout
    This sign indicates that there is a roundabout ahead which is a substitute for intersection.

30. Dangerous Dip

31. Hump or Rough Road

32. Barrier Ahead

33. Speedbreaker

34. Railway Crossing

36. Railway Crossing (Manned)

INFORMATORY SIGNS
=================

Informatory road signs are used to provide important information. These signs are meant to provide information on direction, destination, roadside facilities, etc. to the road user. Following informative road signs helps a driver in saving time, reaching destination without wandering around. These signs are generally facilitators to the driver.

1. Public Telephone
   This sign indicates the availability of Telephone near road.

2. Petrol Pump
   This informatory sign indicates that there is a Petrol Pump ahead.

3. Hospital
   This sign indicates that there is Hospital nearby.

4. Eating Place
   This sign indicates that there is an eating-place in the vicinity.

5. First Aid Post
   This sign shows that there is a First Aid facility nearby which is very useful in case of emergency or accidents.

6. Light Refreshment
   This sign indicates there is facility of Light refreshment nearby on the road.

7. Resting Place
   This sign is erected near motel, lodge or any other facility for resting on the road.

8. No Thorough Road
   This sign is erected at the entry of such roads from which no exit is available.

9. No Thorough Side Road
   This sign indicates that there is no through side road available on the main road.

10. Park this Side
    This sign is erected at destination telling exactly where to park your vehicles.

11. Parking Both sides
    This sign is erected at destination telling exactly where to park your vehicles. This sign indicates that vehicles can be parked on both sides.

12. Parking Scooters & Motorcycles
    This sign is erected at destination, which is meant for parking of scooters and motorcycles.

13. Parking Lot Cycles
    This particular sign indicates that this parking is meant for cycles.

14. Taxi Stand
    This sign is erected at destination which is meant for parking taxis only.

15. Auto Rickshaw Stand
    This sign is erected at destination, which is meant for parking Auto rickshaws.

16. Cycle Rickshaw Stand
    This sign is erected at destination, which is meant for parking Cycle rickshaws.

17. Flood Gauge
    This sign is erected on highways/roads near river, canal, lake etc. This sign indicates the level of water. It also cautions the danger level of the water.

18. Destination Sign
    This sign indicates the direction and distance to various destinations falling on that particular road. These signs are generally installed before intersections.

19. Direction Sign
    This sign shows the direction and distance of the destination/place written on it.
